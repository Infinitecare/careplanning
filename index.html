Care Plan

  • A Nursing Care Plan in aged care is a structured, individualized document that outlines a resident's healthcare needs, goals, and the interventions required to achieve those goals.
  • It is developed collaboratively by nurses, allied health professionals, and sometimes the resident and their family, ensuring a person-centered approach to care.

My Goals

  • I want to prevent pressure injuries by having regular repositioning, using pressure-relieving devices, and having my skin checked often.
  • I want to stay well-hydrated and have a nutritious diet to keep my skin healthy and strong.

My Observations

  • Observe and document the resident’s repositioning schedule, ensuring they are turned or repositioned at appropriate intervals (e.g., every 2 hours for bed-bound residents).
  • Assess and record skin integrity during routine checks, noting any signs of redness, pressure areas, or skin breakdown, and escalate concerns as needed.
  • Monitor and document the resident’s daily fluid intake, ensuring they meet the recommended hydration levels (e.g., 1.5–2 liters per day, unless contraindicated).
  • Observe for signs of dehydration or malnutrition, such as dry skin, poor skin turgor, reduced appetite, or unintended weight loss, and report any concerns to the healthcare team.

Interventions

  • Assist the resident with regular repositioning (e.g., every 2 hours in bed, every 1 hour in a chair) and document each change to prevent prolonged pressure on any one area.
  • Provide and maintain pressure-relieving equipment such as alternating air mattresses, pressure cushions, and heel protectors to reduce the risk of skin breakdown.
  • Conduct daily skin assessments, focusing on pressure-prone areas (e.g., sacrum, heels, elbows), and report any redness, breakdown, or signs of infection to the healthcare team.
  • Regularly check for signs such as dry skin, poor skin turgor, cracked lips, and unintended weight loss, and escalate concerns to the healthcare team.
  • Offer high-protein or fortified drinks as prescribed to support skin health and overall well-being, particularly for residents with reduced appetite.

### **Scenario: Resident Explaining Goals to the Nurse** **Resident:** *"Nurse, I want to talk about my care plan. There are two things that are really important to me."* **Nurse:** *"Of course! I’m here to listen. What would you like to discuss?"* **Resident:** *"First, I want to make sure I don’t get any pressure injuries. I know staying in bed for too long can cause sores, so I want to be repositioned regularly. Also, I’ve heard that using special cushions or mattresses can help. Can you make sure my skin is checked often to catch any early signs of problems?"* **Nurse:** *"That’s a great goal! I’ll ensure we follow a regular repositioning schedule and use pressure-relieving equipment to keep you comfortable. I’ll also check your skin daily and report any concerns immediately."* **Resident:** *"Thank you! The other thing I’m worried about is staying hydrated and eating well. I don’t always feel thirsty, but I know my skin stays healthier when I drink enough water. And I want to make sure I’m getting the right foods to keep my skin strong."* **Nurse:** *"I understand. I’ll remind you to drink water throughout the day and track your fluid intake. I’ll also work with the dietitian to make sure your meals are packed with the right nutrients to support your skin health. If needed, we can add some supplements too."* **Resident:** *"That sounds good! I just want to stay as healthy as possible and avoid any unnecessary problems."* **Nurse:** *"Absolutely, and I’ll be here to support you every step of the way!"*
